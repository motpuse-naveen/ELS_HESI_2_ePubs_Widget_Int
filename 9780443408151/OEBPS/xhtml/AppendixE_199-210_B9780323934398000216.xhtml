Rationales

Question #1 (Multiple Response Select N):

Answer: 3, 4, 6, 7.

Rationale: Hypertension is a risk factor for CAD. An elevated blood pressure (BP) increases cardiac workload which can cause ischemia to the heart muscle in clients with significant atherosclerosis. Shortness of breath with exertion is another sign of increased workload of the heart and decreased perfusion of the cardiac muscle. Dizziness and light-headedness could be a result of decreased blood flow from narrowing of the arteries supplying oxygen to the brain. New onset or worsening of these symptoms could indicate a neurological complication and require further assessment. Weight gain of 25 pounds in a two-month period is a significant amount of weight gain and can put excessive strain on the heart. This weight gain could also be an indication that the patient may be experiencing other complications such as congestive heart failure or kidney injury. The other findings do not require immediate follow-up.

Question #3 (Drop-Down Cloze):

Option 1 Answer: Atrial Fibrillation

Option 2 Answer: Risk for Stroke

Rationale: Risk for stroke is the priority client problem when experiencing atrial fibrillation. Clients have an increased likelihood of developing a clot within the atria due to incomplete emptying of blood from the heart chambers. If the clot leaves the atria, it can travel through the circulatory system to the brain and cause a stroke. Treatment for atrial fibrillation is most likely to cause low blood pressure, not high. The client will require health teaching, but this is not the priority nursing problem. Atrial fibrillation causes increased risk of clotting, not decreased.

Question #6 (Multiple Response—Select All that Apply)

The correct answers are underlined.

  1. 1. “Swelling and redness in my right leg is to be expected and I should elevate it when this occurs.”
    • This is not an appropriate action. This finding could indicate a deep vein thrombosis is present. The client should notify the nurse immediately.
  2. 2. “My pain meds may cause me to be constipated so I should eat oatmeal every day for breakfast.”
    • Taking opioids may lead to constipation. Adding whole grains such as oatmeal will add fiber to the diet and decrease the risk of constipation.
  3. 3. “I should use an electric razor as long as I am taking the blood thinners.”
    • Using an electric razor is advised when clients are prescribed anticoagulants to reduce the risk of bleeding.
  4. 4. “I will be able to drive myself to the rehab center.”
    • It is not advised for the client to drive while taking opioids. Transportation should be provided to the facility.
  5. 5. “My wife will need to go fill my prescriptions at the pharmacy and take them to the rehab center, or I will not have any medicines to take.”
    • The rehabilitation facility will receive the prescriptions for the client prior to discharge. It is the responsibility of the facility to obtain the needed medications for the client. This is usually not a responsibility of the family or client.

Administration of the nebulizer treatment will dilate the bronchioles and increase airflow in and out of the lungs, which will decrease respiratory effort. Next the nurse should consult the respiratory therapist to perform chest physiotherapy. This intervention will break up secretions and mucus and make them easier to expel. Once these have been completed, the nurse can follow through with the other orders.

Question #6 (Drop-Down Rationale):

Answer: Effective dosing of pancreatic enzyme replacement therapy (PERT) is determined by observing the client’s feces. If therapy is sufficient, then the RN will see it sink.

Rationale: Feces from a client who is receiving adequate PERT will sink to the bottom of the toilet. Feces that contain fat will float to the surface of the water in the toilet. Undigested fat in the stool indicates insufficient PERT in clients with CF. This is an easily observable indication of therapeutic medication dosing for clients. PERT will not affect the other body fluids mentioned.
