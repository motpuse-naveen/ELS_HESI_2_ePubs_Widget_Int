Test Administration: Computerized Adaptive Testing

  • • Difficulty level is tailored to the candidate’s ability.
  • • The number of questions for the new Next-Generation NCLEX (NGN) test plan can range from 85 to 150 items. Of these items, 15 are pretest items that are not scored. The time limit for this examination is 5 hours.
  • • Clinical Judgment processes are measured using 18 case-study items (3 unfolding case studies with 6 questions each) and stand-alone items (bowtie or trend).

About Knowledge Test Questions

  • • Multiple Choice
    • — One question with four answer options
    • — Select one option
  • • Multiple Response
    • — Select one or more options
    • — Select all that apply (SATA)
  • • Fill in the Blank
    • — Type in one or more numbers
    • — If rounding is necessary, it is performed at the end of the calculation
  • • Hot Spot
    • — Identify one area in a graphic
    • — Measures skills related to safety, physical assessment, and other procedures and techniques
  • • Chart/Exhibit
    • — Read information in the chart/exhibit to arrive at the correct option
    • — Provides client history, laboratory data, and clinical data on tabs
  • • Ordered Response or Drag-and-Drop
    • — Rank order or move options to provide the correct option
    • — Instructs the individual to order the steps in the correct sequence
  • • Audio Item
    • — Presents the candidate with an audio clip
    • — Individual uses headphones to listen to the question and options
    • — Evaluates a candidate’s competence in certain skills or assessment areas
  • • Graphic Options
    • — May be used as all or part of an individual item, either in the question itself or as part of the response

Next-Generation NCLEX Examination (NGN)–Style Question Types

Question types were developed using the NCSBN Clinical Judgment Measurement Model (NCJMM), which is a valid measurement of a graduate nurse’s level of clinical judgment and decision-making abilities using the context of a client scenario.

The following information was derived from Linda Silvestri and Donna Ignatavicius’s “Next-Generation NCLEX® (NGN) Item Types and Descriptions.” (Examples of these NGN item types will be provided throughout this NCLEX review.)

  • • Bowtie (for stand-alone item only)
    • — Items address more than one of the cognitive skills of the NCJMM in one item. Visually, the item looks like a bowtie. There are five options for selection on the left side and five options on the right side of the bowtie (wells), and four options in the middle well.
  • • Trend (for stand-alone case only)
    • — Trend items address multiple cognitive skills of the NCJMM by requiring the candidate to review information over time. Trend standalones can feature any item response type.
  • • Matrix Multiple Choice
    • — Items have at least 4 and no more than 10 rows. There can be two or three options/columns. Each row must have one response item selected or the candidate will not be allowed to move on to the next item in the exam.
  • • Multiple Response—Select All That Apply
    • — Items allow the candidate to select one or more answer options at a time. There may be only one correct response or multiple correct responses. There will be at least 5 with no more than 10 options, but all 10 options could be correct.
  • • Multiple Response—Select N
    • — Items allow the candidate to select one or more answer options and the question tells the candidate the number of items (N) that can be selected.
  • • Multiple Response—Grouping
    • — Items will be presented in a table and the table will have a minimum of two and a maximum of five groupings. Each grouping has a minimum of two and a maximum of four options, and the number of options for each grouping will be the same. The candidate will need to select at least one option from each grouping.
  • • Matrix Multiple Response
    • — Items will have response columns and each response column could have multiple correct responses. There can be between 2 and 10 columns and 4 to 7 rows. Each column must have one response option selected, but one or more check boxes per column can be selected.
  • • Drop-Down Cloze
    • — Items include one or two sentences of information with one or more drop-down options from which to complete the sentence(s). There will be three to five options in each drop-down. There will be a minimum of one sentence with one drop-down per sentence and a maximum of five sentences with one drop-down per sentence.
  • • Drop-Down Rationale
    • — Items include one sentence with one cause and one effect or one sentence with one cause and two effects. This can be a single dyad (one sentence with two drop-downs) or a single triad (one sentence with three drop-downs each). There can be three to five options in each drop-down.
  • • Drop-Down in Table
    • — Items include a table of information with drop-down options in different parts of the table. There will be a minimum of three columns and three rows and a maximum of five columns and four rows. One column will be a header column. There is one drop-down per row.
  • • Drag and Drop Cloze
    • — Items will have a minimum of 4 and a maximum of 10 selections (known as tokens). There can be one or more response targets to which to drag a token. There is a minimum of one sentence with one target per sentence and a maximum of five sentences with one target per sentence.
  • • Drag and Drop Rationale
    • — Items include one sentence with one cause and one effect or one sentence with one cause and two effects. This can be a single dyad (one sentence with two targets) or a single triad (one sentence with three targets).
  • • Highlight in Text
    • — Items will include a paragraph of information and the candidate will need to select parts of the text based on what the question is asking for the candidate to select. For example, the candidate could be asked to highlight significant information or what is critical for an action. There can be a maximum of 10 options in these question types.
  • • Highlight in Table
    • — Items include a table from which the candidate will need to select parts of the text based on what the question is asking the candidate to select. There can be a maximum of 10 options. The table will include two columns (one being a header) and up to five rows.

General Test-Taking Strategies

  • • Every question must be answered to move on to the next question. Make your best decision if unsure of the answer.
  • • Quickly eliminate the options that do not answer the question.
  • • Reread the question for qualifiers or other words that specify what the question asks.
  • • Decide what makes the responses different from each other.
  • • The options may contain correct information but may not answer the questions.
  • • Only use the information that the question provides. Never say, “But, what if…?” If the question does not state the information, you cannot assume that possible circumstance exists.

Test-Taking Strategies for Your Toolbox

  • • Use ABCs
    • — Clients with airway problems or interventions are priority
    • — Actual CPR = Circulation, Airway, Breathing (CAB)
    • — Acute before chronic
  • • Maslow’s Hierarchy of Needs
    • — Address physiological needs first, followed by safety and security needs, love and belonging needs, self-esteem needs, and, finally, self-actualization needs.
    • — When a physiological need is not addressed in the question, look for the option that addresses safety.
  • • Carefully determine the steps of the nursing process. (Fig. 1.1)
    • — Assessment requires gathering and verification of data.
    • — Analysis requires interpreting data, communicating the diagnoses, and determining the health team’s ability to meet needs.
    • — Planning requires knowledge of prioritization and organization.
    • — Implementation reflects delegation and assignment of tasks.
    • — Evaluation compares actual outcomes with expected outcomes.
  • • Think Safety, Safety, SAFETY!
    • — Perform the least invasive intervention first.
    • — Assess before taking action, unless the patient is in severe distress.
    • — Treat the client, not the machine!
    • — Gather information and perform all relevant actions before calling the health care provider (HCP).
    • — Determine which client to assess first (most at risk, most physiologically unstable).
    • — Follow guidelines for delegating assignments (https://www.ncsbn.org/public-files/NCSBN_Delegation_Guidelines.pdf).
    • — Remember the differences between the scope of practice for the Licensed Registered Nurse (RN), Licensed Practical Nurse (PN), and Unlicensed Assistive Personnel (UAP).

A cyclic flowchart with five circles leading to each other in a clockwise direction shows components of nursing process as follows:•Assess: Gather information about the patient’s condition.•Diagnose: Identify the patient’s problems.•Plan and identify outcomes (goals): Set goals of care and desired outcomes and identify appropriate nursing actions.•Implement: Perform the nursing actions identified in planning.•Evaluate: Determine if goals met and outcomes achieved.

More Test-Taking Strategies

  • • Read the question first (usually the last sentence). Make sure to understand what the question is asking before reading the scenario.
    • — This helps a candidate pick out the pertinent information in the scenario and eliminate distractors.
  • • The question may contain red flag words like all, nothing, never, and always.
  • • Notifying the HCP is a red flag item. Think about the appropriate nursing actions to take prior to notifying the HCP.
  • • Common nursing interventions include:
    • — Assess client status, vital signs, or lab values
    • — Review medication administration
    • — Provide small, frequent feedings
    • — Recommend fluid intake: 3 L/day
    • — Alternate rest with activity
    • — Conserve energy with any activity

Strategies for Success in Answering NCLEX-RN Questions

  • • Essential Steps
    1. 1. Determine if the style of the question is positive (+) or negative (−).
      • — A positive style may ask what the nurse should do or the best or first action to implement.
      • — A negative style may ask what the nurse should avoid, which prescription the nurse should question, or which behavior indicates the need for reteaching the client.
    2. 2. Identify the key words in the question.
      • — Key words include the age of the client, the setting, the timing, a set of symptoms or behaviors, diagnoses, medications, lab values, vital signs.
    3. 3. Rephrase the question.
      • — Ask yourself, “What is the question really asking?”
      • — Before looking at the choices, rephrase the question in your own words. Then answer the question.
    4. 4. Rule out options.
      • — Based on your knowledge, identify any options that are clearly incorrect, and mentally delete them.
      • — Rule out options that delay treatment. Choose options that can be performed immediately.
      • — Differentiate among the remaining options using prior knowledge of the subject and related nursing principles.
    5. 5. Re-read the question with two options left.
      • — Repeat the question in your mind, weighing the two options available.
    6. 6. Answer the question and move on.
      • — Do not second-guess yourself!

Appropriate Knowledge

  • • Use the appropriate knowledge to answer questions.
    • — Do not respond based on:
      • • Your past client care experiences or agency
      • • A familiar phrase or term
      • • “Of course, I would have already…”
      • • What you think is realistic
      • • Your children, pregnancies, parents, elders, personal response to a drug, and so on
    • — Do respond based on:
      • • ABCs
      • • Scientific, behavioral, sociological principles
      • • Principles of teaching/learning
      • • Maslow’s Hierarchy of Needs
      • • Nursing process
    • — Answer based only on what the question asks—no more, no less.
      • • NCLEX-RN ideal hospital
      • • Basic anatomy and physiology
      • • Critical thinking, clinical judgment, and problem solving

    • — Do eat a healthy meal.
    • — Do dress comfortably.
    • — Do allow plenty of time to get there.

Test-Taking Strategy

A word of advice: do not expect to achieve the maximum benefits of this review tool by “cramming” a few days before the examination. It does not work! Instead, organize planned study sessions in an environment that you find relaxing, free of stress, and supportive of the learning process well in advance of taking your NCLEX.

Drag and Drop Rationale

Scenario: The RN is caring for a client admitted with heart failure. The home medication list is obtained.

The medication ______(1)_______ is contraindicated for clients with a history of heart failure and can worsen symptoms such as _________(2)__________.

Home Medication List (Option #1)Symptoms (Option #2)
Furosemide 40 mg PO Daily Exertional fatigue
Potassium chloride 20 mEq PO Daily Hypotension
Pioglitazone 45 mg PO Daily Hyperglycemia
Enalapril 5 mg PO BID Unusual bleeding

Answer: #1 = Pioglitazone, #2 = exertional fatigue.

Rationale: Pioglitazone is a medication used to treat type 2 diabetes and has been known to cause or worsen heart failure. This medication is contraindicated in clients who have heart failure and can lead to worsening symptoms such as exertional fatigue; swelling of the hands, legs, and feet; hypertension; and shortness of breath.
