NCLEX-PN Examination

  • • The test covers essential nursing knowledge (based on entry-level nursing that reflects current nursing practice) in four client needs categories.
  • • The test plan is revised every 3 years, after a practice analysis has been conducted with entry-level practical nurses (PN) (the new test plan was implemented in April 2023).
  • • Information about the NCLEX-PN detailed test plan, including descriptions of content categories and related content for each category, can be found at the National Council of State Boards of Nursing (www.ncsbn.org).
  • • The NCSBN website also presents information for students, FAQs, and examples of alternate formats.

Test Administration: Computerized Adaptive Testing

  • • Difficulty level is tailored to the candidate’s ability.
  • • All candidates must answer a minimum of 85 items.
  • • The maximum number of items during the allotted 5-hour period is 150.
  • • Of these items, 15 are pretest items that are not scored.
  • • Clinical judgment processes are measured using 18 case study items (three unfolding case studies with six questions each).

About Test Item Questions

  • • Multiple Choice
    • — One question
    • — Four choices (answers)
  • • Multiple Response
    • — Select one or more answers from five or six options (can be all choices).
  • • Fill-in-the-Blank
    • — Fill in one or more numbers.
    • — If rounding is necessary, it is performed at the end of the calculation.
  • • Hot Spot
    • — Identify one area on a picture or graphic.
    • — Measures skills related to safety, physical assessment, and other skills.
  • • Chart/Exhibit Format
    • — Problem that requires review of info in the chart/exhibit to arrive at answer
    • — Provides the client history, laboratory data, and clinical data on tabs.
  • • Ordered Response or Drag-and-Drop
    • — Rank order or move options to provide the correct answer.
    • — Presents lists of steps of a procedure and instructs the individual to order the steps in the correct sequence.
  • • Audio Item
    • — Audio clip is heard through headphones.
    • — Evaluates competency in skills or clinical problem solving.
  • • Graphic Options
    • — May be used as all or part of an individual item.
    • — Either in the question itself or as part of the response.

Next Generation NCLEX (NGN) Question Types

Question types were developed using the National Council State Board of Nursing (NCSBN) Clinical Judgment Measurement Model (NCJMM), which is a valid measurement of a graduate practical nurse’s level of clinical judgment and decision-making abilities using the context of a client scenario.

The following information was derived from Linda Silvestri and Donna Ignatavicius’s “Preparing For The Next-Generation NCLEX® (NGN).”

  • • Bowtie (for stand-alone item only)
    • — Items address more than one of the cognitive skills of the NCJMM in one item. Visually, the item looks like a bowtie. There are five options for selection in the left side and five options in the right side of the bowtie (wells), and four options in the middle well.
  • • Trend (for stand-alone case only)
    • — Trend items address multiple cognitive skills of the NCJMM by requiring the candidate to review information over time. Trend standalones can feature any item response type.
  • • Matrix Multiple Choice
    • — Items have at least four rows and no more than 10 rows. There can be two options/columns or three options/columns. Each row must have one response item selected or the candidate will not be allowed to move on to the next item in the exam.
  • • Multiple Response: Select All That Apply
    • — Items allow the candidate to select one or more answer options at a time. There may be only one correct response or multiple correct responses. There will be at least five options with no more than 10 options, but all 10 options could be correct.
  • • Multiple Response: Select N
    • — Items allow the candidate to select one or more answer options and the question tells the candidate the number of items (N) that can be selected.
  • • Multiple Response Grouping
    • — Items will be presented in a table and the table will have a minimum of two groupings and a maximum of five groupings. Each grouping has a minimum of two options and a maximum of four options, and the number of options for each grouping will be the same. The candidate will need to select at least one option from each grouping.
  • • Matrix Multiple Response
    • — Items will have response columns and each response column could have multiple correct responses. There can be between two and 10 columns and four to seven rows. Each column must have one response option selected, but one or more check boxes per column can be selected.
  • • Drop-Down Cloze
    • — Items include one or two sentences of information with one or more drop-down options from which to complete the sentence(s). There will be three to five options in each drop-down. There will be a minimum of one sentence with one drop-down per sentence and a maximum of five sentences with one drop-down per sentence.
  • • Drop-Down Rationale
    • — Items include one sentence with one cause and one effect or one sentence with one cause and two effects. This can be a single dyad (one sentence with two drop-downs) or a single triad (one sentence with three drop-downs each). There can be three to five options in each drop-down.
  • • Drop-Down in Table
    • — Items include a table of information with drop-down options in different parts of the table. There will be a minimum of three columns and three rows and a maximum of five columns and four rows. One column will be a header column. There is one drop-down per row.
  • • Drag-and-Drop Cloze
    • — Items will have a minimum of four selections (known as tokens) and a maximum of 10. There can be one or more response targets to which to drag a token. There is a minimum of one sentence with one target per sentence and a maximum of five sentences with one target per sentence.
  • • Drag-and-Drop Rationale
    • — Items include one sentence with one cause and one effect or one sentence with one cause and two effects. This can be a single dyad (one sentence with two targets) or a single triad (one sentence with three targets).
  • • Highlight in Text
    • — Items will include a paragraph of information, and the candidate will need to select parts of the text based on what the question is asking for the candidate to select. For example, the candidate could be asked to highlight significant information or what is critical for an action. There can be a maximum of 10 options in these question types.
  • • Highlight in Table
    • — Items include a table from which the candidate will need to select parts of the text based on what the question is asking the candidate to select. There can be a maximum of 10 options. The table will include two columns (one being a header) and up to five rows.

(Examples of these NGN item types will be provided throughout the reading.)

Test-Taking Strategies: General Strategies

  • • Every question must be answered to move to the next question, so make your best decision if you are not sure of the answer.
  • • Quickly eliminate the choices that do not answer the question.
  • • Reread the question for qualifiers or other words that specify what the question asks.
  • • Decide what makes the responses different from each other.
  • • The choice may contain correct information but may not answer the question.
  • • Only use the information that the question provides. Never say, “But, what if…?” If the question does not state the information, you cannot assume that possible circumstance exists.

Test-Taking Strategies: For Your Toolbox

  • • Use ABCs
    • — Airway, breathing, circulation
    • • Order of priority
    • — Exception in cardiopulmonary resuscitation (CPR)
    • • Use circulation, airway, breathing (CAB) instead of ABC
    • — Acute before chronic
  • • Maslow’s Hierarchy of Needs
    • — Address physiological needs first, followed by safety and security needs, love and belonging needs, self-esteem needs, and, finally, self-actualization needs.
    • — When a physiological need is not addressed in the question, look for the option that addresses safety.
  • • Carefully Determine the Steps of the Nursing Process (Fig 1.1)
    • — Data collection questions involve gathering data.
    • — Planning questions require collaboration and assisting with the development of a plan of care.
    • — Implementation questions address issues of assisting with organizing and managing care and communicating nursing interventions thoroughly and accurately.
    • — Evaluation questions focus on comparing the actual outcomes of care with the expected outcomes and on communicating and documenting findings.

• Assess: Gather information about the patient’s condition. • Diagnose: Identify the patient’s problems. • Plan and identify outcomes (goals): Set goals of care and desired outcomes and identify appropriate nursing actions. • Implement: Perform the nursing actions identified in planning. • Evaluate: Determine if goals met and outcomes achieved.

  • • Safety, Safety, SAFETY
    • — Choose a nursing action that would prevent harm to the client.
    • — Collect data before taking action when appropriate. If client is in severe distress, take action first.
    • — Have all necessary information and take all possible relevant actions before calling the health care provider (HCP).
    • — Start with the least invasive intervention.
    • — Determine priority (which client is most at risk/physiologically unstable).
    • — Follow the guidelines for delegation (https://www.ncsbn.org/public-files/NCSBN_Delegation_Guidelines.pdf). Remember the difference between the role of licensed practical nurse (PN) and the role of unlicensed assistive personnel (UAP).

More Test-Taking Strategies

  • • Read the question first (usually the last sentence). Make sure to have a good understanding of what is being asked before reading the scenario.
    • — This helps a candidate pick out the pertinent information in the scenario and look beyond the unnecessary information.
  • • The question may contain red flag words like all, nothing, never, and always.
  • • Notifying the HCP is a red flag item.
  • • — Determine the appropriate nursing actions to take prior to notifying the HCP.
  • • Common interventions include:
    • — Assess client status, vital signs, and/or laboratory values
    • — Review medication administration information
    • — Small, frequent feedings
    • — Recommended fluid intake: 3 L/day
    • — Alternate rest with activity
    • — Conserve energy with any activity
  • • Reinforce teaching points like:
    • — Risk factors: modifiable versus nonmodifiable
    • — Prevention and wellness promotion
    • — New medications/self-care instructions
    • — Client empowerment
    • — Anticipatory guidance
    • — Incorporating education into client’s lifestyle, culture, and spiritual beliefs

Strategies for Success in Answering NCLEX-PN Questions: Essential Steps

  • 1. Determine if the style of the question is positive (+) or negative (–).
    • — A positive style may ask what the nurse should do or the best or first action to implement.
    • — A negative style may ask what the nurse should avoid, which prescription the nurse should question, or which behavior indicates the need for reteaching the client.
  • 2. Find the keywords in the question.
    • — The age of the client, the setting, the timing, a set of symptoms or behaviors, diagnoses, medications, laboratory values, and vital signs
    • — For example, the nursing actions for a 10-year-old, 1-day postop client are different from those for a 70-year-old, 1-hour postop client.
  • 3. Rephrase the question in your own words.
    • — This helps eliminate nonessential information in the question and helps you determine the correct answer.
    • — Ask yourself, “What is the question really asking?”
    • — Before looking at the options, rephrase the question in your own words. Then answer the question.
  • 4. Rule out options.
    • — Based on your knowledge, identify any options that are clearly incorrect.
    • — Mentally mark through those options on the computer monitor.
    • — Rule out options that delay treatment (e.g., continue to monitor, reassess in 15 minutes). Choose options that can be performed immediately.
    • — Differentiate among the remaining options using prior knowledge of the subject and related nursing principles, such as roles of the PN, nursing process, ABCs, and Maslow’s hierarchy.
  • 5. Reread the question with two options left.
  • 6.  Answer question and move on.

Use Appropriate Knowledge

  • • Use appropriate knowledge to answer questions.
    • — Do not respond based on:
      • • Your past client care experiences or agency
      • • A familiar phrase or term
      • • “Of course, I would have already …”
      • • What you think is realistic
      • • Your children, pregnancies, parents, elders, personal response to a drug
    • — Do respond based on:
      • • ABCs
      • • Scientific, behavioral, and sociological principles
      • • Principles of teaching/learning
      • • Maslow’s Hierarchy of Needs
      • • Nursing process
    • — Answer based only on what the question asks—no more, no less.
      • • Ideal hospital
      • • Basic anatomy and physiology
      • • Critical thinking

Practice Questions
  1. 1. A new client in the prenatal clinic tells the PN she cannot afford to maintain her heart-healthy diabetic diet. Which is the most appropriate nursing intervention by the PN?
    1. 1. Provide an educational film about the value of nutrition in pregnancy.
    2. 2. Inform the mother how inadequate nutrition affects the infant’s health.
    3. 3. Assist client in finding a resource for providing adequate nutrition.
    4. 4. Refer client to a dietician responsible for clients diagnosed with diabetes.

Correct Answer: 3

Rationale

Assisting the client in finding resources to provide adequate nutrition is most appropriate and most helpful to the client. The other nursing interventions are not the most beneficial in addressing the client’s stated concern.

  1. 2. The PN is preparing a client for discharge. Which method would best show the client understands self-care regarding the ability to perform insulin injections at home?
    1. 1. View the client reading the information booklet on diabetes.
    2. 2. Have the PN demonstrate the steps of insulin injection.
    3. 3. Reinforce teaching and observe the client’s self-injection technique.
    4. 4. Provide the client with free access to an online interactive program on injection techniques.

Correct Answer: 3

Rationale

Reinforcing teaching and observing the client directly will allow the PN to determine if mastery of the skill has been obtained and provide an opportunity to affirm the skill. The other nursing interventions are not the most beneficial to determine client understanding of discharge teaching.

  1. 3. A client who is 32 weeks pregnant did not eat before her prenatal visit and had a small amount of ketones in her urine specimen. Which would the PN suspect?
    1. 1. Early-onset gestational diabetes
    2. 2. An indication for a potential preeclampsia condition
    3. 3. Symptomatic of a urinary tract infection
    4. 4. Normal pregnancy changes in carbohydrate metabolism

Correct Answer: 4

Rationale

A missed meal often causes presence of ketones in the urine. It indicates inadequate carbohydrate intake. The ketones do not indicate gestational diabetes, preeclampsia, or urinary tract infection.

  1. 4. Which would the PN anticipate as the most serious complication for a neonate delivered by a client who is addicted to heroin?
    1. 1. Hyperbilirubinemia
    2. 2. Postmaturity of the infant
    3. 3. Psychological addiction to heroin
    4. 4. Probability of meconium aspiration

Correct Answer: 4

Rationale

There is a very high probability of meconium aspiration in the infant of a mother addicted to heroin. Efforts to preserve airway and breathing are the highest priority. The other answer options are lower priority.

  1. 5. The PN observes the relationship between a mother and her 4-year-old child. Which behavior would the PN consider indicative of positive parenting? (Select all that apply.)
    1. 1. Child maintains eye contact with the parent.
    2. 2. Child is shown how to pet the dog nicely.
    3. 3. Parent asks the child to help with dusting the room.
    4. 4. Parent says, “Don’t do that. It’s wrong.”
    5. 5. Parent asks the child, “Would you like a bedtime story?”

Correct Answer(s): 1, 2, 3, 5

Rationale

A child who maintains eye contact with the parent, treats animals with kindness, helps with household chores, and is open to hearing a bedtime story from the parent is a child raised by a parent with positive parenting skills. These actions demonstrate autonomy. A parent who tells a child they are wrong is not exhibiting positive parenting skills.

  1. 6. A postpartum client admitted for observation of gestational hypertension angrily ejects her partner from the room. When the PN enters the room, the client is having a seizure. Which action would the PN take? (Select all that apply.)
    1. 1. Place something soft in the client’s mouth to protect the tongue from being bitten.
    2. 2. Pull the emergency call light.
    3. 3. Notify the registered nurse (RN) immediately.
    4. 4. Administer a dose of calcium gluconate.
    5. 5. Give oxytocin 10 mg IM per protocol.

Correct Answer(s): 2, 3

Rationale

The PN should pull the emergency call light for assistance and notify the RN immediately when a client is having a seizure. Nothing should be placed in the client’s mouth. Calcium gluconate reverses magnesium toxicity and does not need to be administered. The client does not need to be given oxytocin.

  1. 7. The PN needs to prepare an 8-year-old child for an IV insertion. Which intervention is appropriate?
    1. 1. Encourage the child to use guided imagery to cope.
    2. 2. Place the client in the treatment room, not the child’s room.
    3. 3. Apply a lidocaine-based cream just before inserting the IV catheter.
    4. 4. Ask the parents to leave the room while performing the procedure.

Correct Answer: 2

Rationale

The child’s room is a safe zone, and painful treatments should not be performed in the child’s room. Guided imagery may be too advanced for a child of this age. Lidocaine-based creams work more effectively when left on for a longer period of time, preferably 30 to 60 minutes. Asking the parents to leave is inappropriate and can increase the child’s anxiety.

  1. 8. An 18-year-old woman is being discharged after delivering a healthy baby. Which behavior does the PN need to correct to reduce the risk of sudden infant death syndrome (SIDS)?
    1. 1. Always place infants on their backs to sleep.
    2. 2. Room sharing has been shown to decrease SIDS.
    3. 3. Keep the crib free of stuffed animals and crib pads.
    4. 4. Sleeping with the baby can alert the mother to changes.

Correct Answer: 4

Rationale

Sleeping with the baby increases the risk of injury and SIDS in infants. The other answer options should be implemented to decrease the risk of SIDS.

Test-Taking Strategy

A word of advice: Do not expect to achieve maximum benefits of this review tool by “cramming” a few days before the examination. It does not work! Instead, organize planned study sessions in an environment that you find relaxing, free of stress, and supportive of the learning process well before taking your NCLEX.

Drag-and-Drop Rationale

Scenario: The PN is caring for a client admitted with heart failure. The home medication list is obtained.

The medication ______(1)_______ is contraindicated for clients with a history of heart failure and can worsen symptoms such as _________(2)__________.

HOME MED LIST (OPTION #1)SYMPTOMS (OPTION #2)
Furosemide 40 mg PO daily Exertional fatigue
Potassium chloride 20 mEq PO daily Hypotension
Pioglitazone 45 mg PO daily Hyperglycemia
Enalapril 5 mg PO BID Unusual bleeding

Answer: #1 = Pioglitazone 45 mg PO daily, #2 = Exertional fatigue

Rationale: Pioglitazone is a medication used to treat type 2 diabetes and has been known to cause or worsen heart failure. This medication is contraindicated in clients with heart failure and can lead to worsening symptoms such as exertional fatigue; swelling of the hands, legs, and feet; hypertension; and shortness of breath.
